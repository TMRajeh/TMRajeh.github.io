About Me

I am Dr. Taha M. Rajeh, a Senior Lecturer and Distinguished Associate Research Fellow at Stirling College, Chengdu University, China. I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the School of Computing and Artificial Intelligence at Southwest Jiaotong University.

My research focuses on deep reinforcement learning, urban computing, traffic flow prediction, anomaly detection, time series analysis, and computer vision—with applications in smart cities, transportation systems, and infrastructure.

I have published widely in high-impact, Q1 journals including IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems, Knowledge-Based Systems, International Journal of Machine Learning and Cybernetics, and Applied Intelligence. As a Professional Member of the China Computer Federation (CCF), I actively serve the AI community as a reviewer, conference session chair, and invited speaker.

My current work explores vision-based reinforcement learning, cooperative and incremental learning, transfer learning, and the integration of large language models with reinforcement learning—advancing intelligent solutions across domains from smart cities and transportation to healthcare.
